## About This Portfolio

This portfolio showcases my practical GRC skills through a simulated engagement with **GreenLeaf E-Commerce**, a fictional 50-employee online retail company. I acted as the GRC Analyst, identifying risks, writing policies, conducting audits, assessing vendors, and building security awareness programs.

**The Problem:** GreenLeaf had no formal security program. Customer data was at risk, employees had never been trained, and the company was non-compliant with GDPR.

**The Solution:** I built a complete GRC framework from scratch – from Risk Register to Incident Response Plan to Security Awareness Training.

**Skills Demonstrated:**
- Risk Identification & Scoring (Inherent vs Residual)
- Policy & Standards Drafting (NIST-aligned)
- Compliance Mapping (ISO 27001, NIST CSF, PCI DSS)
- Internal Auditing & Non-Conformity Reporting
- Vendor Risk Assessment (TPRM)
- Data Privacy (DPIA)
- Incident Response Planning & Tabletop Exercises
- GRC Tooling (CISO Assistant, Google Looker Studio)

## What I Learned

1. **Risk management is not just about identifying risks – it's about prioritising them.** The 5x5 matrix helped me separate Critical risks from High and Medium risks, focusing resources where they matter most.

2. **Compliance is about alignment, not just paperwork.** Mapping risks to ISO 27001 and NIST CSF helped me understand that controls are not arbitrary – they are responses to specific threats.

3. **Communication is everything.** The Executive Dashboard was the most valuable deliverable because it translated technical risk into business language for the CEO.

4. **Vendors are an extension of your security posture.** PaySecure taught me that a single weak vendor can compromise your entire security program.

5. **Awareness training is the cheapest and most effective control.** A $500 training platform can prevent a $50,000 phishing attack.
